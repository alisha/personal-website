Community

Code for Boston (2014–Present)

I'm a member of Code for Boston, a Code for America organization where volunteers collaborate on social and civic projects to improve the lives of Bostonians.

I've worked on Ungentry, a map of urbanization and gentrification patterns in Boston. I also made a guide to help local animal shelters make Twitter bots that tweet about their adoptable pets.

Check out my Ignite talk at Code for Boston's National Day of Civic Hacking 2015 event!


Weston High School Computer Science Team (2012–2016)

President (2015–2016)

Every week, students gather to code and work on projects in the Computer Science Team. As President, I transformed our group into a more collaborative environment as we worked on a single coding project for the first time and attended competitions. At the 2015 Providence College High School Programming Contest, I was one of four students to represent our school, and our team earned 3rd Place. Members also competed individually in monthly USA Computing Olympiad contests, of which I'm a Silver Division competitor.


Weston High School Science Team (2012–2016)

Co-Captain (2015–2016), Secretary (2014–2015)

The Science Team participates in monthly Science Olympiad regional competitons and annual state competitions. As Co-Captain, I assigned team members to rounds for these competitions and helped them get organized and prepared for their rounds. I also got the team involved in our first Science Olympiad invitationals and first USABO qualifying exams.


Weston High School Math Team (2012–2016)

Co-Captain (2015–2016)

The Math Team participates in competitions like the New England Math League contests, Purple Comet, Harvard MIT Math Tournament, and MA Math League meets. As co-captain, I expanded our community by fostering an inclusive and diverse environment. In addition, I worked with the other co-captains to assign people to rounds for our monthly competitions.


Girls Who Code Weston (2014–2015)

Mentor (2014–2015)

Every week, I mentored our future programmers: middle school girls! I helped them organize ideas and problem solve as they made games in Scratch.


Weston High School Principal's Advisory Council (2013–2015)

Class of 2016 Representative (2013–2015)

PAC is a committee of students and faculty who meet with our principal every month to discuss ways we can improve our school. In addition to attending monthly meetings, I was on the Library Renovations Committee, where I sent out surveys and led focus groups to help transform our library into a learning commons.


Weston High School Student Council (2012–2015)

Class of 2016 Representative (2012–2015)

During my time on Student Council, I helped to plan school-wide events that strengthened our community and helped others. For example, Student Council is in charge of the annual Spring Fling event, a fundraiser where high school clubs run booths for children. We've donated to Camp No Limits and City Year Boston in the past.
