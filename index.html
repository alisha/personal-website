I'm Alisha Ukani

I'm a high school senior.
I like to make things.

I attend Weston High School in Weston, MA, where I lead the Math, Science, and Computer Science Teams. Outside of school, I help organize CodeDay Boston (a 24-hour hackathon for high school students), and lead cool projects at Code for Boston's weekly hack nights.

I really like coding, especially when it helps other people. I built QuiziCal, a web app that helps teachers communicate and collaborate about dates for assessments. This past summer at Conjuguemos, I created a game to help students learn verb conjugation in foreign languages. I'm now a Software Development Intern for the Metropolitan Area Planning Council. In my free time, I'm building a mobile app to combat food insecurity.

I conduct microbiology research at Harvard University, where I'm investigating bacteria volume regulation. In the past, I've developed an algorithm to find degenerate genome motifs and visualized the CRISPR/Cas System.
